What It Does

After every meeting, push a Telegram digest with:

  1. 2 content observations — what happened, anchored to team purpose/goals
  2. 2 performance observations — individual + team dynamics
  3. 5th member perspective — counterweights what the team needs (pushes action when over-analyzing, questions us when rushing)

The loop: transcript → AI + Telos context → digest → members correct via voice note → corrections update context → next digest is better.

What Matters for Building

Anchored or it's Firefly

What made Oseas say “you have my attention” wasn’t the summary — it was that the AI knew what we’re trying to do and measured us against it. Without the team’s purpose/goals/challenges as context, it’s a meeting summary. With them, it says “you spent 80% on architecture and 0% on the customer — and the focus group is in 10 days.”

Corrections can't be optional

The AI will misinterpret — “I think we should” becomes “decided.” If that accumulates uncorrected, every future digest builds on errors and people stop speaking freely. V0 rule: “I noticed” language, never “this happened.” Convener spends 10 min after each meeting correcting.

Context has to evolve

If week 1’s context is all the system knows in week 4, the output goes generic and people stop reading. Corrections feed back into context files. The Telos changes as we change. This is the core mechanism, not a V2 feature.

The Differentiator

People buy the 5th team member — the coach, the digest, the perspective shift. But the reason they stay is because it actually knows them. It gets better. It remembers.

Week 1, any AI tool gives a decent summary. Week 8, every other tool still treats each meeting like it’s the first. Ours knows Leo raised this 6 weeks ago, Oseas killed it in week 3, and it’s back because conditions changed.

Zero moat

Transcript → digest. Everyone does this. GPT, Claude, Firefly, Otter.

Some moat

Context-aware digest. Telos + history make the output specific and personal.

The differentiator

Context that stays correct over time with human validation. Nobody does this. It’s why they use us and not the next AI tool.

The design problem we need to own

How do humans validate AI’s understanding — naturally, quickly, without it feeling like work?

Forms → nobody fills them. Dashboards → nobody visits. Our answer: you talk to it. Voice note to the 5th member: “you got this right, but that wasn’t a decision.” Next time, it’s smarter.

The correction loop isn’t a side feature. It’s the core interaction that makes the context trustworthy and the AI useful over time.

V0 tests three things

  1. Do people actually correct the AI? (Or ignore it?)
  2. Does week 2’s digest improve from week 1’s corrections?
  3. Does it feel like talking to a teammate, not filling out a form?

What We're NOT Building in V0

No dashboard, no automatic transcripts, no individual Telos, no knowledge graph, no access control. Manual upload, text files, shared context, 4 trusted people.
