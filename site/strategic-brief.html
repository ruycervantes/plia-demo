Executive Summary

  1. Build AI POC analyzing Izen's past production communications

The Problem (Izen's Perspective)

Surface Problem

“Our UK partnerships keep going over budget. Cultural differences, communication breakdowns, their difficult work style.”

Actual Problem (What Data Shows)

Systematic dysfunction across three dimensions:

1. “IT” — Systems / Task (Impersonal)

  • No coordinated work methodology between teams
  • Unclear roles and responsibilities
  • Insufficient preparation time
  • Head office disconnected from production crews
  • Learning from past failures not systematized

2. “WE” — Relationships (Interpersonal)

  • Poor communication (unclear requests, no inquiry)
  • Conflicts avoided rather than resolved
  • Lack of team cohesion
  • Cultural/language barriers unaddressed
  • Us-vs-them dynamics (Spain vs UK)

3. “I” — Leadership (Personal)

  • Leadership distant and evasive
  • Executives avoid decisions, seek scapegoats
  • Variable commitment (staff vs contractors)
  • Blame culture vs accountability culture

The Pattern (From Email Analysis)

Based on preliminary analysis and Axialent's diagnostic interviews:

  • Trust starts high, collapses around Week 3
  • Defensive communication spikes
  • Escalation culture (problems go UP not ACROSS)
  • Silo behavior (internal over cross-team communication)
  • Budget overruns become inevitable by Week 5–6

The POC Strategy

Objective

Convince José Velasco that:

  1. Izen has an expensive invisible problem (consciousness)
  2. AI can make patterns visible and actionable (capability)
  3. Stoic represents an investment opportunity (strategic value)
  4. Partnership/JV makes sense for scaling (business model)

Approach: Forensic Analysis

Phase 1: Data Gathering (Current — Monday meeting)

  • Determine email access (Izen only vs Izen + Clapperboard)
  • Identify available productions for analysis
  • Secure budget/outcome data
  • Define data scope and limitations

Phase 2: Pattern Analysis (2–4 weeks)

  • Analyze past productions using Five Behaviors framework:
    • Trust (vulnerability vs defensiveness)
    • Conflict (healthy debate vs avoidance/escalation)
    • Commitment (clear decisions vs revisiting)
    • Accountability (peer-to-peer vs triangulation)
    • Results (team focus vs silo behavior)
  • Compare successful vs troubled productions
  • Correlate communication patterns to financial outcomes
  • Identify predictive indicators (Week 3 collapse, early warnings)

Phase 3: The Reveal (Presentation to Velasco + Sara)

Create compelling narrative:

  1. “Here's what's killing your margins” (pattern visualization)
  2. “Here's how it happens” (the systematic collapse)
  3. “Here's what it's costing you” (financial analysis)
  4. “Here's your contribution” (not just blame UK)
  5. “Here's the solution” (technology + human interventions)
  6. “Here's the opportunity” (investment pitch)

Deliverable Options

Option A: Minimal POC (Fastest)

  • Analyze limited email sample (10–50 emails)
  • Demonstrate pattern detection works
  • Show proof of concept
  • Create curiosity for deeper analysis
  • Timeline: 1–2 weeks

Option B: Credible POC (Recommended)

  • Analyze 2–3 complete productions
  • Compare successful vs troubled
  • Show correlation to financial outcomes
  • Demonstrate baseline + predictive capability
  • Timeline: 3–4 weeks

Option C: Full Experience (Most Convincing)

  • Analyze 5+ productions comprehensively
  • Real-time monitoring of current production
  • Dashboard with live metrics
  • Full “Pattern Detective” demonstration
  • Timeline: 6–8 weeks

Recommendation: Start with Option B, design for expansion to Option C.

Critical Success Factors

For POC to Work

  • Use IZEN's actual data (not hypothetical examples) — Must analyze real productions, show their actual patterns, use their own words/emails. Undeniable because it's theirs.
  • Show THEIR contribution (not just blame UK) — Both sides contribute to dysfunction. System problem, not personality problem. Empowering vs blaming frame. “Here's what you can control.”
  • Financially framed (speak Velasco's language) — Connect patterns to money lost. Show ROI clearly. Compare investment cost to savings. Make business case, not therapy case.
  • Create urgency (problem is NOW) — Current productions at risk. Money bleeding RIGHT NOW. Window for intervention closing. Cost of delay is real.
  • Show path forward (solution + opportunity) — Not just problem diagnosis. Technology enables new approach. Human + AI together. Bigger than Izen alone.

The Delicate Balance

Must achieve:

  • Show serious problem (create urgency)
  • Prove technology works (build credibility)
  • Position as opportunity (not just sale)
  • Ask for investment (the real ask)

Without:

  • Making them feel incompetent (defensive response)
  • Overpromising AI magic (credibility loss)
  • Seeming opportunistic (trust loss)
  • Creating “if so good, why need money?” paradox

Key Risks & Mitigation

Risk 1

Insufficient Data

Risk: Not enough email volume or productions to prove patterns

Impact: POC seems like cherry-picked example, not systematic proof

Mitigation:

  • Transparent about data limitations
  • Frame as “proof of concept” not “final answer”
  • Offer deeper analysis contingent on initial interest
  • Use Axialent interview data to supplement

Risk 2

Defensive Response

Risk: Velasco/Sara reject mirror (“that's not us”)

Impact: Kill the conversation before investment discussion

Mitigation:

  • Lead with successful productions (they can do this)
  • Frame as system problem (both sides contribute)
  • Use empowering language (“here's what you control”)
  • Avoid blame, emphasize opportunity

Risk 3

“Just Fix It” vs Investment

Risk: They want consulting service, not investment opportunity

Impact: Become vendor not partner, lose strategic positioning

Mitigation:

  • Position as bigger than Izen problem (industry transformation)
  • Show limited capacity (can't scale without investment)
  • Frame JV as win-win (they get showcase + returns)
  • Make investment attractive on its own merits

Risk 4

Technology Not Ready

Risk: AI doesn't detect patterns reliably yet

Impact: Credibility destroyed, opportunity lost

Mitigation:

  • Conservative claims (pattern detection not magic prediction)
  • Complement with Axialent human expertise
  • Frame as augmentation not replacement
  • Be transparent about current capabilities

Risk 5

Timeline Pressure

Risk: Not enough time to build credible POC

Impact: Rush leads to poor quality, unconvincing demo

Mitigation:

  • Start with minimal viable (Option A)
  • Design for expansion (can deepen if interested)
  • Use “Pattern Detective” concept as future vision
  • Deliver what's promised, promise what's deliverable
